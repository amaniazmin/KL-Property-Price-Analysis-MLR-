# Kuala Lumpur Property Price Analysis: A Multiple Linear Regression Approach
### Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Scikit-Learn | Method: Multiple Linear Regression (MLR)

## Project Overview
This research project investigates the determinants of residential property prices in Kuala Lumpur. By applying Multiple Linear Regression, the study quantifies the impact of structural attributes (size, rooms, furnishing) and locational advantages on market valuation, providing data-driven insights for stakeholders and policymakers.

## Key Research Objectives
* **Identify Significance**: Determine which structural factors (e.g., number of rooms, property size) most significantly affect housing prices.
* **Quantify Impact**: Measure the correlation between furnishing levels and final market price.
* **Policy Recommendations**: Offer evidence-based suggestions for affordable housing development in urban centers.

## Methodology & Workflow
1. **Data Cleansing**: Addressed missing values and outliers to ensure the reliability of the regression results.
2. **Feature Engineering**: Processed variables such as property size, room count, and furnishing status.
3. **Statistical Modeling**: Implemented Multiple Linear Regression to analyze the relationship between independent variables and the dependent variable (Price).
4. **Validation**: Performed diagnostic checks to ensure the model satisfies the assumptions of linear regression.

## Findings Summary
The results indicate that **property size** is the most significant predictor of price, followed by the **number of rooms** and **furnishing condition**. While locational factors are present, structural attributes remain the primary value drivers in this specific dataset.
